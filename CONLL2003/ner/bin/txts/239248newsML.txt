GDR REPORT - India finishes flat as markets rebound.

LONDON 1996-12-06

Indian global depositary receipts (GDRs) ended little changed in thin London trade after coming off in line with world markets on inflation comments from Federal Reserve chairman Alan Greenspan.

"The rally in the Indian domestic market was pretty thin, but Greenspan put the dampers on London and the market wasn't going anywhere in any case," said a trader.

The HSBC James Capel Indian GDR index  JCEAI closed up 0.31 points, 0.58 percent, at 54.46  points, with the premium over the local market at 14.28 percent compared with yesterday's 13.96 percent.

The index had been down 0.80 to 1.00 percent earlier in the day.

The Bombay Stock Exchange 30-share index rose 13.98 points or 0.50 percent to 2,826.20.

"We saw market makers cautiously marking prices higher in the afternoon as the Dow recovered but there was no volume as investors are unwilling to take positions," said a trader.

The Capel Korea GDR Index ended down 1.02 points, 1.66 percent, at 60.49, a premium of 28.85 percent over the local market after the Korea Composite Index fell 6.41 points to 699.89.

The Capel Taiwan GDR Index ended down 2.15 points, 2.27 percent, at 92.67, a premium of 3.09 percent after the Taiwan Weighted Index dropped 112.68 points or 1.63 percent to 6,806.81.
