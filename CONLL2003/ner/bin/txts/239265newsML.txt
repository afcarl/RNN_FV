Rugby Union-Former Ireland captain Kingston retire.

DUBLIN 1996-12-06

Former Ireland hooker and team captain, Terry Kingston, on Friday announced his retirement from representative rugby union.

"I had a most enjoyable career....the time has come for me to step aside from the representative scene. I want to devote more time to my job," Kingston said.

Kingston, 33, is a director of a computer accessories and systems company in his native city of Cork in southern Ireland.

He had the distinction of playing in all three World Cup competitions, he captained Ireland in the World Cup of 1995 in South Africa, and won 29 international caps.

Kingston played his last match for Irish provincial side Munster against Australia at Thomond Park, Limerick, on November 26. He captained Munster when they defeated Australia in October 1992.
