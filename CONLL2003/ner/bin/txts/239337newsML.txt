U.S. welcomes Caspian pipeline deal.

WASHINGTON 1996-12-06

The U.S. government warmly welcomed the signing Friday of a deal to build a $1.5 billion oil pipeline linking Kazakhstan and Russia.

"The United States looks forward to working with all the involved parties to contribute to the success of this project," State Department spokesman Nicholas Burns said in a statement.

Italian oil company AGIP SpA announced in Rome that the deal on the Caspian Pipeline Consortium (CPC) had been signed in Moscow between the states of Russia, Kazakhstan and Oman and eight international oil companies including U.S.-based Mobil.
