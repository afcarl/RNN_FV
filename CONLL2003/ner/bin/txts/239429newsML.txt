U.S.  October consumer credit rose $2.2 billion.

WASHINGTON 1996-12-06

U.S. consumer installment credit rose $2.2 billion in October at a 2.3 percent annual rate, after falling a revised $700 million in September at a 0.8 percent annual rate, the Federal Reserve said Friday.

Previously, the Fed said September consumer credit outstanding had fallen much more sharply by $2.6 billion.
