PRESS DIGEST - Bosnia - Dec 6.

SARAJEVO 1996-12-06

These are the leading stories in the Sarajevo press on Friday. Reuters has not verified these stories and does not vouch for their accuracy.

OSLOBODJENJE

- The European Commission and the Bosnian Presidency signed an agreement allocating an additional $190 million to Bosnian reconstruction, the paper says.

- The contest for $60 million worth of Turkish credits is still open for Bosnian companies, an adviser to the Bosnian Prime Minister said. Companies will have three years to pay off the credit offered by the Turkexim company.

- The status of the Bosnian insurance bureau in the International Association of Insurance Companies will be resolved at the association's general assembly in June 1997.

DNEVNI AVAZ

- The Islamic society of Bosnia and Hercegovina charges the Bosnian Serb republic with destroying 15 mosques in the northern Bosnian town of Banja Luka, the daily quotes Moslem MINA agency as saying.

VECERNJE NOVINE

- Return of a small group of people, even if we call it deportation, does not significantly spoil relationship between Bosnia and Germany, Ismet Grbo, a spokesman to the rulling Moslem Party of Democratic Action (SDA) tells a news conference on Thursday.  Return of those people is even desirable, he says referring to the German decision to deport Bosnian refugees back to their homes.
