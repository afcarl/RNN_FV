PRESS DIGEST - Bulgaria - Dec 6.

SOFIA, Dec 6 (Reter) These are some of the main stories in Sofia newspapers on Friday. Reuters has not verified these stories and does not vouch for their accuracy.

24 CHASA

-- The lev will rise against the dollar if a currency board is introduced in the country, a Bulgaria's central bank senior official said.

-- Bulgaria may declare a default on payments of its foreign debt if the budget does not receive the required 115 billion lev credit from the central bank, Finance Minister Dimitar Kostov said.

-- Bulgaria's state Reconstruction and Development Fund said it would extend some $10 million to secure payments for the import of 120,000 tonnes of Argentine wheat and 20,000 tonnes of wheat from the European Union in January.

-- Bulgaria's cabinet said it would propose to parliament to take a 13.5 percent stake in the Greece-based regional Black Sea Trade and Development Bank.

TRUD

-- Bulgaria's cabinet will ban the privatisation of 25-30 military hardware plants in the next three years, Industry Minister Lyubomir Dachev said.

PARI

-- The Bulgarian National Bank (BNB) will demand explanations from all commercial banks with long foreign currency positions, a BNB official said.

-- Bulgaria's Interior Minister Nikolai Dobrev is expected to present to the cabinet a report outlining cases of corruption in the government, Industry minister Lybomir Dachev said.

STANDART

-- Bulgaria's central bank will put in circulation a new 10,000 lev note on Monday, which is the largest denomination so far, a BNB senior official said.

KONTINENT

-- A senior official of the private Bulgarian Trade and Industrial bank was arrested on Tuesday on charges of siphoning some 10 million levs from accounts of the Plovdiv municipal privatisation agency, police officials said.
