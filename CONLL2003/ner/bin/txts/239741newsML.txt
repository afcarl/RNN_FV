PRESS DIGEST - Romania - Dec 6.

BUCHAREST 1996-12-06

Here are highlights of stories in Romania's press on Friday. Reuters has not verified these stories and does not vouch for their accuracy:

Business:

ROMANIA LIBER

-  Romgaz RA gas utility cut gas supplies to thermal power plants by 1.0 million cubic metres per day.

ADEVARUL

- President Emil Constantinescu requested parliament to start checks on the Finance Ministry over a bad administration of public money.

- Trade union confederation CNSLR-Fratia asks the new government to boost wages by 30 percent by January.

- Romania's new government will renegotiate a stand-by loan accord with the International Monetary Fund (IMF). Central bank governor Mugur Isarescu said Romania needed external financing worth $1.5 billion in 1997.

ZIUA

- The IMF is expected to set new criteria for Romania to qualify for loans.

- The Bucharest stock exchange closed the week with an insignificant turnover level.

CURIERUL NATIONAL

- A post-privatisation fund was created in Romania and it can finance the growth of small and medium-sized enterpries.

CRONICA ROMANA

- The outgoing government of Prime Minister Nicolae Vacaroiu took steps which delayed reforms, future finance minister Mircea Ciumara says in an interview.

EVENIMENTUL ZILEI

- Romania is the world's tenth largest cigarette importer.  

General:

ROMANIA LIBERA

- Ousted president Ion Iliescu wants to create a new party which will not include members of his Party of Social Democracy (PDSR) allegedly involved in corruption cases, the paper says.

- Romania fulfils all conditions to integrate in NATO , President Emil Constantinescu said in interview.

ADEVARUL

- Editorial presents the governing programme of Prime Minister-designate Victor Ciorbea.

- Ion Diaconescu, leader of the National Peasant Party (PNT-CD), was elected head of the Democratic Convention (CDR).

- The newspaper publishes the text of the coalition accord to be signed today by the CDR, the Social Democratic Union and the Hungarian Democratic Union in a ceremony attended by Constantinescu.

EVENIMENTUL ZILEI

- The PDSR holds an extraordinary national conference on December 20 to change the party's statute.

LIBERTATEA

- The newspaper publishes excerpts from a book on corruption in Romania, written by General Ion Pitulescu, former head of the general police inspectorate.

ZIUA

- Romania's secret services are lead by the KGB, says Ion Mihai Pacepa, a former head of the Securitate secret police who defected to the United States in 1977.

- The new government will have 20 ministries and 26 ministers and state secretaries, said Cirobea.

- Fratia trade union leader Pavel Todoran said his confederation will not sign a social accord with the new government unless wages are not increased by 30 percent starting January 1997. ($=3.617 lei) -- Bucharest Newsroom 40-1 3120264
