PRESS DIGEST - Croatia - Dec 6.

ZAGREB 1996-12-06

Following are the main stories in Croatian newspapers on Friday.

VJESNIK

- The government to spend 3 billion kuna on rehabilitation of Privredna Banka Zagreb.

- U.S. trade department officials at a meeting with Croatian ambassador express concern over what they perceive as sealing-off of Croatia's market to U.S. goods.

- The State Judicial Council confirms the dismissal of the Supreme Court president Krunislav Olujic at a closed door session.

- Are Bosnian Federation Croats to end up without several tens of thousands of their homes in Bosnian Army territory? The flats have been proclaimed as abandoned and Muslim officials and journalists have moved in.

- Croatia Banka expects to maintain this year's profit at 25 million kuna this year and increase it by 25 percent next year.

- A group of 36 Croatian Serb refugees to be repatriated by UNHCR in the next few days and to enter Croatia at the Bajakovo border crossing.

VECERNJI LIST

- Croatian newsstands currently offer 2,163 different newspapers and magazines, out of those 809 are published in Croatia.

- Milk production to be subsidised with between 0.55 and 0.80 kuna per liter and other incentives for cattle breeders also planned.

- Government will become majority owner of Privredna Banka by taking over most of its bad assets and recapitalising it.

SLOBODNA DALMACIJA

- Padua-based Banca Antoniana offers to form a new bank to salvage what is left of the failed Banco di Credito di Trieste with a consortium of Croatian and Slovenian banks

- All state officials and institutions put under scrutiny in the Sabor as a heated debate on findings of the state audit continues.

- Italian firm Costanzo invests in Sibenik's free trade zone Podi. -- Zagreb Newsroom, 385-1-4557075
