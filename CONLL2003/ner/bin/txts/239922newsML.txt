S.Korea eyes major hike in software production.

SEOUL 1996-12-06

South Korea's software industry plans to increase the value of software production to $17.2 billion by 2001 from an estimated $3.8 billion this year, a government statement said on Friday.

The Ministry of Information and Communication said investment in research and development would be raised to 12 percent of production value in 2001, from five percent in 1995.

It projected a rise in software exports to $2.5 billion by 2001 from an estimated $30 million this year, the ministry said.

Exports of multimedia products were expected to reach $500 million by 2001, it added.
