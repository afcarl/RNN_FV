NYMEX to raise natgas, electricity margins Monday.

NEW YORK 1996-12-06

The New York Mercantile Exchange said it will increase margins on its three natural gas and two electricity futures contracts, effective at the close of business Monday.

For Henry Hub gas futures, the initial margin will be increased to $3,800 from $3,300 for clearing members; to $4,180 from $3,630 for members; and to $5,130 from $4,455 for customers.

For Permian Basin gas futures, the initial margin will be raised to $2,500 from $1,500 for clearing members; to $2,750 from $1,650 for members; and to $3,375 from $2,025 for customers.

For Alberta gas futures, the initial margin will be increased to $2,000 from $1,000 for clearing members; to $2,200 from $1,100 for members; and to $2,700 from $1,350 for customers.

Electricity futures at the California-Oregon border will be raised to $1,400 from $1,100 for clearing members; $1,540 from $1,210 for members; and to $1,890 from $1,485 for customers.

Palo Verde electricity futures will rise to $1,400 from $1,100 for clearing members; to $1,540 from $1,210 for members; and to $1,890 from $1,485 for customers.

Inter-month spreads involving the Henry Hub spot month will be increased to $1,500 from $1,000 for clearing members; to $1,650 from $1,100 for members; and to $2,025 from $1,350 for customers.

For all other months, spreads will be raised to $500 from $400 for clearing members; to $550 from $440 for members; and to $675 from $540 for customers.
