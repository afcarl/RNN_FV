Belgian cen.bank confirms higher growth forecast.

BRUSSELS 1996-12-06

Belgium's central bank confirmed on Friday it revised its forecast for 1996 real Gross Domestic Product (GDP) growth to 1.4 percent from 1.2 percent.

The Belgian Primary Dealers Association's newsletter said on Monday that the central bank had raised its 1996 forecast to 1.4 percent, from 1.2 percent in August.

The 15 primary dealers on Monday also revised their 1996 GDP growth forecast to 1.5 percent from 1.3 percent in September.

The European Commission in November forecast the Belgian economy would grow 1.4 percent in 1996 and 2.2 percent in 1997.
