Bremen coffee prices ease, buyers take advantage.

BREMEN 1996-12-06

Outright coffee prices in the Bremen market were $1.50-2.0 off on the week per 50 kg fob.

Traders said this was the net effect of lower futures although New York last reversed losses on short-covering after five days of losses in a row amid fund liquidations.

Buyers took advantage of the lower prices, motivated by forecasts Brazil's coming coffee crop should fall from 1996/97 due to weather patterns and falling uses of farm inputs.

Arabica differentials were steady to higher.

Robusta buyers kept an eye on quality and transport problems arising from unusually heavy rainfall in Vietnam.
