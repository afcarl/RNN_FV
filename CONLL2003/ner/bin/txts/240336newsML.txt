PRESS DIGEST - Pakistan - December 6.

These are the leading stories in the Pakistani press on Friday. Reuters has not verified these stories and does not vouch for their accuracy.

DAWN

- Pakistan's caretaker government has amended election laws to cut poll expenses, stop defection and stop loan or tax defaulters from contesting elections.

- Foreign Minister Sahabzada Yaqoob Khan, during his visit to New Delhi later this month, is expected to hold informal talks with his Indian counterpart I.K. Gujral and also other leaders on issues concerning the sub-continent.

- Pakistan said the U.S. State Department's objection to Chinese nuclear cooperation to Pakistan for peaceful purposes was discrimination towards Pakistan.

- The State (central) bank of Pakistan has asked all the banks and development financial institutions to send their balance sheets of the last 12 years.

- The government is reviewing its gold policy to provide a level-playing field to traders.

- The textile quota policy for calendar 1998 will be announced next month.

- Ousted prime minister Benazir Bhutto has alleged that the president's men were negotiating with imprisoned criminals for their release on a condition that they would attack her Pakistan People's Party leaders and disrupt their meetings. BUSINESS RECORDER

- China has agreed in principle to provide Pakistan with another nuclear power plant like Chashma but the question of financing was still to be decided.

FINANCIAL POST

- The president of the Federation of Pakistan Chambers of Commerce and Industry Ilyas Bilour said the economy had plunged to its lowest point.

THE NEWS

- Former Pakistani prime minister Nawaz Sharif promised to uphold any IMF deal reached by the caretaker government if he wins elections on February 3.

- Deadlock over restoration of cellular telephones in Sindh persists as the government insists that mobile telephone operators forego compensation for suspension of services in Karachi and Hyderabad.

- Pakistan Muslim League (PML) party has collected 30 million rupees from those who applied to get PML tickets for the National Assembly (lower house) or provicial assembly seats.
