PRESS DIGEST - Sri Lanka Newspapers - December 6.

These are the leading stories in the Sri Lankan press on Friday. Reuters has not verified these stories and does not vouch for their accuracy.

---

VEERAKESARI

Housing Minister Nimal Siripala de Silva tells parliament that President Chandrika Kumaratunga's residence to be shifted out of Colombo to Kotte for security reasons. Kumaratunga has said Tamil Tiger rebels are planning to assassinate her.

---

DAILY NEWS

Defence Ministry says 50 suspected Tamil Tiger rebel suicide bombers arrested in Vavuniya welfare camps.

---

THE ISLAND

Sri Lanka Telecom engineers union decides not to provide interconnections to two wireless local loop operators, Telia Lanka and Lanka Bell.

---

LANKADEEPA

Lack of proper leadership and command and control blamed for army's July debacle in which Tamil Tiger rebels overran Mullaitivu brigade-sized base with more than 1,000 soldiers in six hours.

---

DIVAINA

Security forces recover stock of arms hidden in rice mill in Batticaloa.

---

DINAMINA

Head of nurses labour union Muruttettuwe Ananda says external forces and some newspapers trying to make political capital by provoking strike.
