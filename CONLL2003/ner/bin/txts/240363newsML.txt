Indian gilts steady in increased volume, call easy.

BOMBAY 1996-12-06

Indian government securities prices were steady on reporting Friday in increased volume trade, dealers said.

"Volume on the National Stock Exchange rose to 2.38 billion rupess on Friday, compared to previous day's 1.37 billion rupees," a dealer said.

The securities market opened with selling pressure and closed with buying interest, he said.

The 13.55 percent 2001 stock saw a volume of 400 million rupees in the price band of 100.32 rupess to 100.34 rupees, unchanged from it's previous levels, he said

The 13.50 percent 1997 stock fell nine basis points to 102.75 rupees on Friday, he said.

The newly auctioned 364-day treasury bill was in limelight on Friday with a volume of 950 million rupees at a yield of 10.20 to 10.30 percent, he said.

The treasury bill maturing in January was in demand on Friday with 4th January, 11th January, 25th January maturity treasury bills dealt at a yield of 6.47, 6.79 and 6.74 percent respectively with a combined volume of 300 million rupees, against the previous yield of 6.85 percent for January maturity treasury bills, he said.

There was no action in call money market on the reporting Friday, dealers said. There were some stray deals below 0.25 percent levels, while the three-day call money rates hovered in a wide range of 2.0 percent to 6.0 percent.

Dealers expect call rates to open in the range of 4.0 percent to 5.0 percent on Saturday. "The call rate will slip to 2.0 percent to 3.0 percent towards the close on Saturday," a dealer said.

"The mid December may see some tighness in the call rates," a dealer at state-run bank said.
