INDIAN HEADLINE STOCKS - factors to watch - Dec 6.

BOMBAY 1996-12-06

Following are some of the main factors affecting Indian stock markets on Friday morning:

NEWS OUTLOOK:

**  Fledgling Indian telecom firm BPL Cellular said on Thursday it was withdrawing its $200 million issue of American Depository Receipts (ADR) -- the country's first U.S. offering.

BPL Cellular said in a statement it was forced to withdraw the issue due to poor sentiment for India. The announcement came after repeated delays in the pricing of the issue.

**  Finance Minister P. Chidambaram has ruled out any dilution of the minimum alternate tax (MAT) levied through the Union Budget 1996/97 (April-March), the Observer reports.

**  Securities firm ING Barings warned investors they must be cautious with first quarter 1997 allocations for India.

In a December strategy piece titled, "Where Angels Fear to Tread," ING said the Indian economy was poised for a shakeout similar to the one witnessed in the early nineties.

**  The Observer says German automobile major BMW has reiterated that it would not invest in the car project in India unless the government changed its policy on indigenisation.

BMW which had earlier planned to start production in 1996, recently decided to delay the automobile project rather than take a big investment decision at this point of time, the paper reports.  

**  The Indian rupee  INR= opened at 35.80/81 per dollar on Friday against the previous close of 35.78/80. Friday's range seen at 35./78/83 rupees to a dollar, dealers said.

**  MARKETS:

**  Indian shares recorded fresh gains in a technical recovery across major bourses on Thursday, with the Bombay Stock Exchange ((BSE)  .BSESN rising rallying 67/16 points or over 2 percent to end at 2,812.22.

The 50-stock National Stock Exchange (NSE)  .NSEI jumped 1.8 percent to close at 802.04.
