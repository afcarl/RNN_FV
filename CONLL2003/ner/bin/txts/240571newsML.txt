BANK OF GREECE GOVERNOR TO PRESENT EURO ON DEC 13.

ATHENS 1996-12-06

The governor of the Greek central bank, Lucas Papademos, will present the initial design of the seven denominations of euro, EU's future single currency, the bank said in a statement.

The euro designs were approved by the board of directors of the European Monetary Institute (EMI) on December 3, it said.

Alexandre Lamfalussi, the president of EMI. will present the leaders of the EU member countries with the euro in Dublin on December 13, it said.

A similar presentation will take place at the headquarters of the EMI in Frankfurt.

Euro will be available in denominations of 5, 10, 20, 50, 100, 200 and 500, it added.

Euros will be in circulation around the end of 2001 in accordance with a decision taken by the European council of December 1995. --Dimitris Kontogiannis, Athens Newsroom +301 3311812-4
