PRESS DIGEST - New York Times business - Dec 6.

NEW YORK 1996-12-06

The New York Times reported the following business stories on Friday:

- Newmont Mining Corp announced an unsolicited bid of $2 billion in stock for Santa Fe Pacific Gold Corp.

- Retailers reported fair to good gains in November sales.

- The bond market went into its steepest dive in more than three months in heavy trading, after a steady climb for much of the last few weeks.

- The New York Stock Exchange proposed making the circuit breakers that halt trading when prices plunge less restrictive than they are now.

- With brisk sales of Jeeps and mini-vans producing a torrent of cash, Chrysler said it would raise its quarterly dividend.

- In a restructuring that would reshape Japanese telecommunications, Nippon Telegraph and Telephone agreed to split into a long distance service provider and two regional companies for local service.

- The successor to Mutual Benefit Life Insurance, the biggest American insurance company to fail, announced a settlement with creditors and policyholders.

- Productivity fell a revised three-tenths of one percent in the third quarter, and first time jobless claims fell last week, hinting inflationary pressures could lie ahead.

- Henkel said Loctite accepted its sweetened bid of $61 a share or $1.27 billion.

- The N.A.S.D. expelled Stratton Oakmont from the securities industry, calling it "an ongoing risk to the investing public.''

- Basketball star Michael Jordan and Michael Jordan the Westinghouse Electric chief executive, who is also chairman of the United Negro College Fund, have been brought together in a new advertising campaign for the fund.

- McCann-Erickson bought Webber Group of Cambridge, Mass., and an interactive sibling, Thunder House.
