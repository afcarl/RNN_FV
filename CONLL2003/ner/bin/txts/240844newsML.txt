Greenspan remarks spook Swedish debt and crown.

1515 GMT - Swedish debt yields ended the week sharply higher as Fed Chairman Alan Greenspan's warning of "irrational exuberance" in assets markets spooked investors worldwide.

The crown tracked a weaker dollar and softenend versus the mark.

Dealers hoped that the focus next week would shift to Swedish fundamentals with November CPI figures seen paving the way for lower yields. * Six-month T-bills up two basis points at 4.10 percent * Ten-year bond yields rise twelve basis points to 7.04 percent * The mark firms to 4.4035 crowns versus 4.3800 late Thursday
