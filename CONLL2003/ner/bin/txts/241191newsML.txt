PRESS DIGEST - HK Newspaper Headlines - Dec 6.

HONG KONG 1996-12-06

These are some of the leading stories in the Hong Kong press on Friday. Reuter has not verified these stories and does not vouch for their accuracy.

-- MING PAO DAILY -- Governor Chris Patten said Hong Kong British passport holders who also have a passport of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region would not receive British consular protection after the handover.

-- TIN TIN DAILY NEWS -- The Housing Committee's 1997/98 budget had a deficit of HK$7.6 billion due to a drop in the sales of Home Ownership Scheme flats.

-- TA KUNG PAO -- Xinhua News Agency Hong Kong branch deputy director Zhang Junsheng said holders of British passports obtained under the British Nationality Selection Scheme would face certain limitations when taking on public positions.

-- A nation-wide Hong Kong knowledge contest was launched jointly by television stations of major Chinese cities.

-- SING TAO DAILY NEWS -- "Drug queen" Poon Lai-lin was sentenced to jail for 30 years for drug trafficking. Her husband is facing execution in China.

-- Research showed foreign investment in Hong Kong increased even though over half of foreign investors thought the political climate was turning worse and labour cost was going up.

-- HONGKONG ECONOMIC TIMES -- Shares of Denway Investment rose on speculation that Peugeot was to pull out of the firms' loss-making joint-venture, Guangzhou Peugeot Automobile Co.

-- SOUTH CHINA MORNING POST -- Dickson Concepts' planned expasion of its retail operations into the United States inched forward with court approval for Barney's of New York to reimburse a due diligence investigation.

-- China faces a road construction funding shortage of US$16 billion over the next five years, but hopes to offset the shortfall with foreign capital.

-- HONGKONG STANDARD -- Hong Kong was making headway in resolving its part of a Sino-U.S. dispute over textile quotas.
