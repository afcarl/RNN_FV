PRESS DIGEST - Philippine newspapers - Dec 6.

MANILA 1996-12-06

These are the leading stories in Manila newspapers on Friday. Reuters has not verified these stories and does not vouch for their accuracy.

- President Ramos rejects calls by Congress leaders to give people a Christmas gift by rolling back fuel prices to previous levels. (PHILIPPINE STAR)

- President Ramos tells his Transportation Minister Amado Lagdameo to "stay put" despite refusal of members of Congress' Commission on Appointments to confirm the minister due to alleged irregularities in a 120 billion peso sale of government property. (MALAYA)

- The Court of Tax Appeals orders the Bureau of Customs to release 9,949 metric tonnes of sugar valued at 80 million pesos after it approved a surety bond of 83.7 million pesos to be posted by the importer, New Frontier Sugar Corp of Iloilo in central Philippines. (MANILA BULLETIN)

- Two groups to hold separate press conferences, one to launch a signature campaign to change the constitution allowing Philippine President Fidel Ramos to seek re-election and another opposing efforts to amend it. (TODAY/CHRONICLE)

- American popstar Michael Jackson arrives in Manila to hold two concerts (ALL NEWSPAPERS)

BUSINESS STORIES

- Annualised inflation rate declines to 4.5 percent in November from 4.7 percent in the previous month due to lower food prices. (ALL NEWSPAPERS)

- Uniwide Holdings Inc negotiates with Petron Corp for the establishment of convenience stores in strategic gasoline stations nationwide. (BUSINESS DAILY)

- All Asia Capital Corp says seven universal banks are likely candidates for mergers or consolidations after the Central Bank raised minimum capital requirements. (MALAYA)

- Asea One Corp, an affiliate of Malaysian carmaker Proton Pilipinas Corp, says it will undertake the development of a 97-hectare industrial park in the northern province of Pangasinan costing 1.75 billion pesos. (TIMES JOURNAL)
