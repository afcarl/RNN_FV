PRESS DIGEST - Egypt - Dec 6.

CAIRO 1996-12-06

These are the leading stories in the Egyptian press on Friday. Reuters has not verified these stories and does not vouch for their accuracy.

AL-AHRAM

- Agreement in principle was reached in a meeting headed by Egyptian Prime Minister Kamal Ganzouri for 71 new tourism projects, worth 5 billion pounds and providing 70,000 job opportunities.

- Suzanne Mubarak says in a speech at Britain's Oxford University that Egypt is entering a new era of revival and progress, and that Egypt is working to achieve peace. She said the development plan has given priority to supporting and assisting women.

AL-AKHBAR

- Kamel Darwish, a World Bank vice president, said Egyptian President Hosni Mubarak has succeeded in bringing Egypt out of an economic crisis.

- Iraqi delegation to visit Egypt next Thursday to discuss importing Egyptian food and medicines.

- Egyptian Foreign Minister Amr Moussa holds talks in Britain about the peace process.

- Mubarak calls for points of view to be brought closer together; A meeting between Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu and Palestinian President Yasser Arafat in Cairo is expected in the next few days.

AL-GOMHURIA

- Moussa gives Mubarak a report on his talks with Netanyahu. Moussa said he informed the Israeli prime minister about Egypt's position concerning the stalled peace process and stressed the need for Israel to stick to the principle of land for peace.
