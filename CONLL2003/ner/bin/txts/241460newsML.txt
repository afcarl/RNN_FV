Danish central bank sells CDs at unchanged rate.

COPENHAGEN 1996-12-06

Denmark's central bank said on Friday it offered to sell 14-day certificates of deposit (CDs) at a fixed yield of 3.50 percent, unchanged from the previous two-week offer.

The bank steers money market rates and liquidity through its 14-day certificates of deposit (CDs) deals and state paper repos.

The central bank discount and key deposit rates were unchanged at 3.25 percent. --Steve Weizman, Copenhagen newsroom, 45-33-969650
