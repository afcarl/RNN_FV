PRESS DIGEST - Ireland - December 6.

DUBLIN 1996-12-06

Following are the highlights of stories reported in the Irish press on Friday.

IRISH TIMES

- The health authorities have written to 105 patients treated by a doctor who has since been diagnosed as HIV positive.

- The government was facing a new deadline to secure the publication of the controversial Price Waterhouse report last night as Prime Minister John Bruton admitted that he has personlly approached Ben Dunne for financial support for Fine Gael.

- Community confidence in the RUC has fallen dramatically since last year and there has been a sharp rise in the proportion of both Catholic and Protestants favouring reform of the force according to a survey carried out by the Northern Ireland Police Authority.

- Ireland has delivered on its Florence summit promise to produce an outline draft treaty for the EU, the prime minister said on Thursday.

- As the government seeks to negotiate tight control on public sector pay in the talks on a new national agreement, the ESRI, an economic research body, has strongly recommended much tighter control of spending in this area.

- More Irish private investors should be allowed to buy shares in state assets, opposition leader Bertie Ahern said on Thursday.

IRISH INDEPENDENT

- The government may ask a High Court judge to make the final decision on what parts of the Price Waterhouse report should be published.

- Opposition leader Mary Harney demanded that a judicial inquiry be set up immediately if the Dunne family was not willing to release the details of the controversial Price Waterhouse report.

- A Catholic school was damaged in a suspected arson attack last night, the fourth in Ballymena, Northern Ireland, in 24 hours.

- Sunday working would be optional for retail staff under an opposition private members bill published on Thursday.

- Almost four out of every five retail financial services companies expect a decrease in their staffing levels over the next few years.
