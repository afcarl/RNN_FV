PRESS DIGEST - Egypt - Dec 7.

CAIRO 1996-12-07

These are the leading stories in the Egyptian press on Saturday. Reuters has not verified these stories and does not vouch for their accuracy.

AL-AHRAM

- Egyptian President Hosni Mubarak and Palestinian President Yasser Arafat will discuss in Cairo today ways to put the peace process back on track. Mubarak and Foreign Minister Amr Moussa will discuss Moussa's talks with Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu. Mubarak has sent a second letter to Netanyahu.

- Egypt calls on the new American administration to stick to the role of mediator in the peace process, as well as to the principles of Madrid and land for peace. Moussa says from London that he is looking forward to working with Madeleine Albright, nominated as new U.S. secretary of state.

- Egyptian delegation ends visit to Britain where they held talks with British officials on how to coordinate efforts to fight terrorism.

- European Union's peace coordinator to arrive in Cairo on Saturday and hold talks with Mubarak on Sunday.

AKHBAR AL-YOUM

- Mubarak intends to end all remaining obstacles to investment activity. There is readiness to abolish any law or article which inhibits the interests of investors.

- Egyptian source denies there were efforts underway to hold a meeting in Cairo between Netanyahu and Arafat in coming days.

AL-GOMHURIA

- Egyptian Prime Minister Kamal Ganzouri and his Polish counterpart Wlodzimierz Cimoszewicz will hold talks in Cairo on increasing economic cooperation.

- Public Enterprise Minister Atef Obeid says there is no turning back from the country's privatisation drive.
