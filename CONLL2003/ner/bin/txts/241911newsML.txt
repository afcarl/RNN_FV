PRESS DIGEST - Bangladesh Newspapers - Dec 7.

These are some of the leading stories in the Bangladesh press on Saturday. Reuters has not verified these stories and does not vouch for their accuracy.

---

THE DAILY STAR

Prime Minister Sheikh Hasina said her government would not give any appointment or promotion to the public servants on the basis of political allegience.

Bangladesh Nationalist Party (BNP) has warned that a water sharing accord should not be signed in exchange for transit facility to India.

---

THE INDEPENDENT

BNP Secretary-General Abdul Mannan Bhuiyan has urged all concerned to forge greater unity to launch a massive movement against what he said the present "autocratic" government.

Jamaat-e-Islami Secretary-General Moulana Matiur Rahman Nizami has called upon the political parties to exercise tolerance and stop mudslinging against each other in order to give democracy a firm footing in this country.

---

BANGLADESH OBSERVER

Members of the Out-market (kerb) Share Traders Association have urged the government to protect interest of small investors, and said they will submit a memorandum to the prime minister on Monday highlighting their grievances and demands.

Foreign Minister Abdus Samad Azad has congratulated Mrs. Madeline Albright on her appointment as the Secretary of State of the United States.

---

FINANCIAL EXPRESS

Traders have been importing goods from India braving a hazardous and very uncertain situation in the absence of an insurance policy covering risks on cargo.

BNP Sceretary-General Abdul Mannan Bhuiyan has alleged that cronies of ruling Awami League were behind the crash of the stock market. "The collaborators of Awami League have looted the money of small investors in a pre-planned way," he said.
