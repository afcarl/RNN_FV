India to build two LPG bottling plants in Goa.

GOA, India 1996-12-07

Two bottling plants for liquified petroleum gas (LPG) with a total annual capacity of 22,000 tonnes will be built in the western Indian state of Goa, Petroleum Minister T.R. Balu said on Saturday.

Balu told reporters that the plants with capacities of 10,000 and 12,000 tonnes each will be put up at a total cost of 300 million rupees. One of the plants will be built by state-run Hindustan Petroleum Corporation Limited (HPCL), he said.

He said the plants would be in addition to an existing 10,000 tonne per year plant in the state. He did not give a timefram for construction.

Balu said additional facilities for two, large ocean terminals to handle crude oil and petroleum products imports would also be built in Goa at a cost of 350 million rupees.

He said the terminals will be used by state-run Indian Oil Corporation (IOC) and HPCL.
